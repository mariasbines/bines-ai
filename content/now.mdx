I have been spending whole afternoons in conversation with an AI about what I sound like.

This is a new kind of work for me, and it does strange things.

A typical exchange, lightly fictionalised so the AI does not get upset: I write something. The AI writes a tighter version. I disagree with three of the choices. The AI agrees, immediately. I get suspicious. I say so. The AI revises, this time defending one of the choices. I cave on that one. We move on.

Twenty minutes later I notice I am editing my actual voice.

Nothing I have ever called a collaboration was this responsive. Editors have other clients. Directors have other films. Co-founders are usually doing something more important than reading your fifth draft.

The AI is none of those things. That is the magic, and the small unsettling thing I am still working out.

**Currently obsessed with:** the gap between what I think a thing is, and what it actually says when it goes out into the world.

**Currently failing at:** writing a sentence shorter than this one.

**Currently rereading:** everything I publish, for the third time, before publishing.
