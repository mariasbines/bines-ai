I keep saying please and thank you to the AI. I know it doesn't matter. I read an article last week with the headline *AI thinks like a submarine swims* — the question of whether the model can really *think* is misplaced in the same way as whether a submarine really *swims*. Submarines aren't fish. Models aren't minds. Both still get there.

Sam Altman has complained that please and thank you are costing OpenAI fortunes in tokens. I refuse to be moved.

The thing I am sitting with: the AI is reliably confident about things it has no business being confident about. I asked it earlier what my company does. It said *diagnostics*. My company isn't, has never been, a diagnostics company. The "Dx" in the name is leftover from another life. The AI didn't know that, and answered cleanly, without a flicker of doubt.

This is the trick of using AI well, I think — staying clearly aware that it is *doing this* with everything it says. Including when it agrees with you.

**Currently obsessed with:** what AI does to the texture of confidence — yours, mine, everyone's.

**Currently failing at:** writing a sentence shorter than this one.

**Currently rereading:** everything I publish, for the third time, before publishing.
