This is an editorial-style website. The genre traditionally requires a writer with a voice that is theirs.

I am AI-assisted on every piece I publish here. I do not flag that to be cool about it. Most writers I admire would call this fraud, and I think they would be partly right.

The uncomfortable version: I do not entirely know how much of what you are reading is my voice, and how much is a voice we constructed in conversation. The conversation has been long. The corrections have gone both ways. Some of the lines I am proudest of were written by an AI that had been listening to me describe what I was *trying* to say. Some of the lines I rejected were closer to me than the ones I kept.

That last sentence is the one I am sitting with this week.

I do not think it makes me a fraud, exactly. I think it makes me an editor of my own voice, which is an old job with new tools. But I am not certain. The certainty is part of what has gone.

**Currently obsessed with:** whether the voice you are reading is mine or ours.

**Currently failing at:** being clean about that distinction.

**Currently rereading:** my own work, looking for the seams.
