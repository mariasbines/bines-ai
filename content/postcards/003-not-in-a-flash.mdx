Told an audience this month to *"be the architect, not the bystander."* It sounded great in the piece. I had a control room. Dashboards. Brakes that could be pulled. Drama.

I am a bystander on something right now. I had a meeting about it. I shared an article. My calendar synced with everyone else's calendars and we all agreed to keep an eye on it.

The architect line was for the conference. The bystander part is the daily life of approximately everyone, including me.
