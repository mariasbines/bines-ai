Jay and I bought an old camper. *I,* technically. Jay fixed it up real nice — the toilet flushes, the rear camera keeps its fat ass out of the shoulder. The interior is riotously red. My Russian friend Olga clocked it as Cold War Eastern Bloc apartment décor, and that, I think, settles it.

We named it Morris. The exact opposite of Le Maurice in Paris, where Jay and I had our first weekend away. The dream is being chauffeured around the west coast of Ireland with Woody and Clyde, two maniacal French bulldogs. Clyde is the wild one. Friends say he looks like Ed from Madagascar.

Jay calls it a lawn ornament for now. The British keep asking why I need more than one vehicle. *Ve-hic-el,* the second syllable a small accusation.

I am very excited about Spring.
